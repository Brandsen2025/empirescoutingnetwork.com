Player Overview

Rodrigo Nicanor Mora Núñez was a Uruguayan forward whose career is defined by his legendary spell at River Plate. After strong seasons at Defensor Sporting and Peñarol, he joined River Plate in 2012. He truly flourished under manager Marcelo Gallardo, becoming the heart and soul of the attack. While not a prolific scorer, he was a "big game" player, known for his relentless pressing, intelligent movement, and crucial goals in continental competitions. He was a key part of the team that won the 2014 Copa Sudamericana, the 2015 Copa Libertadores, and the 2018 Copa Libertadores, among many other titles. His career was unfortunately cut short by a debilitating hip necrosis.
