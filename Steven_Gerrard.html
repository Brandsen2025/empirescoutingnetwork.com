üåç Global Impact Analysis

Steven Gerrard is widely regarded as one of the greatest midfielders in football history and the greatest player to ever wear a Liverpool shirt. A one-club legend who spent 17 years at Anfield (1998-2015), Gerrard made 710 appearances and scored 186 goals - a phenomenal return for a midfielder. His trophy cabinet includes the UEFA Champions League (2005), UEFA Cup (2001), FA Cup (2001, 2006), and League Cup (2001, 2003, 2012). The captain who embodied Liverpool's spirit, Gerrard's performances in the 2005 Champions League final (the Miracle of Istanbul) and the 2006 FA Cup final are the stuff of legend - both matches saw him inspire extraordinary comebacks. A complete midfielder who could do everything - tackle, pass, shoot, lead - he scored 120 Premier League goals and provided 92 assists across 504 league appearances. Despite winning every major honour except the Premier League (finishing runners-up three times), he never left Liverpool, turning down moves to Chelsea and Real Madrid. For England, he earned 114 caps and scored 21 goals, captaining his country at multiple tournaments. His individual accolades include PFA Player of the Year (2006), FWA Footballer of the Year (2009), and UEFA Club Footballer of the Year (2005). After Liverpool, he had a brief stint at LA Galaxy before becoming a manager. Steven Gerrard is not just Liverpool's greatest - he's one of the Premier League's all-time icons.
