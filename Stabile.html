Career Overview

Guillermo Stábile was a prolific Argentine centre forward, famous for his performances at Huracán and his subsequent successful spells in Italy and France. He became the first top scorer in FIFA World Cup history, scoring eight goals in the inaugural 1930 tournament across only four matches. As a manager, he led Argentina to six South American Championships and Racing Club to three Argentine league titles.
