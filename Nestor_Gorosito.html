Player Overview

Néstor Fabián Gorosito, universally known as "Pipo," enjoyed a lengthy playing career as a technical attacking midfielder in Argentine football before becoming a respected manager. His playing days spanned from the early 1980s through late 1990s, featuring stints at numerous Argentine clubs including San Lorenzo, Unión de Santa Fe, and Independiente. Known for his creativity, goal-scoring ability from midfield, and football intelligence, he was the type of skilled playmaker common in Argentine football. He won league titles and established himself as a reliable attacking force. After retirement, his tactical understanding led to a successful coaching career, including winning the 2007 Copa Sudamericana with Arsenal de Sarandí and managing clubs like San Lorenzo, Tigre, and Banfield.
