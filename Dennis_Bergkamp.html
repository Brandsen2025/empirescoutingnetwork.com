Analysis

Bergkamp's psychology is defined by an absolute obsession with technical perfection. He was famously termed "The Iceman" for his lack of emotional outburst and surgical precision. His aviophobia (fear of flying) restricted his European appearances but paradoxically deepened his focus on domestic excellence. He did not play for the crowd; he played for the purity of the action.

Jul 4, 1998 | "Dennis Bergkamp!"
Netherlands 2-1 Argentina (WC Quarter-Final). In the 90th minute, Bergkamp controls a 60-yard pass from Frank de Boer, flicks it inside Ayala, and volleys it home. One of the greatest goals in World Cup history.

Newcastle 0-2 Arsenal. Bergkamp receives the ball with his back to goal, flicks it one way around Nikos Dabizas, spins the other way, and slots it home. Voted the best goal in Premier League history.
Aug 27, 1997 | The Hat-Trick
Leicester 3-3 Arsenal. Bergkamp scores arguably the greatest hat-trick ever seen: a curler from the edge of the box, a chip over the keeper, and a juggle-and-finish that defied physics.
May 15, 2004 | The Invincibles
Arsenal 2-1 Leicester. Bergkamp captains Arsenal in their final game of the unbeaten season. His through ball sets up Vieira for the winner, sealing immortality.
